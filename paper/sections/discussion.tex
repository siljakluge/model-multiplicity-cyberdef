\section{Discussion}

This section will interpret the final results, including the relationship between prediction conflict and explanation variability, limitations of the current setup, and implications for cyber defense workflows. The interpretation should also distinguish aggregate similarity from operational equivalence. Recent work on model stealing through model multiplicity shows that surrogates with comparable fidelity can still differ substantially in deployment-relevant properties~\cite{baltz2026model}. The same caution applies to intrusion-detection multiplicity: if models in the Rashomon set achieve similar F1 scores but disagree on specific flows or produce unstable explanations, then the selected detector should not be treated as the uniquely justified representative of the learned security policy.
